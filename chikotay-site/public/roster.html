01 · Founder & CEO
David Droga
Australian · Born in Thredbo, NSW
Active — CEO, Accenture Song

Why Him

The single most awarded creative at Cannes Lions in history — over 70 Lions including six Grand Prix and four Titanium. But what makes Droga the CEO, not the CCO, is that he's already done both. He founded Droga5 from nothing and built it into an agency named Agency of the Year 11 times. Then he took a $10.6 billion division of Accenture and rebranded it as Accenture Song. He understands business at global scale AND creative at the highest level. He was worldwide CCO of Publicis at 34. He's the rare founder who can sit in a boardroom with Fortune 500 CEOs and in a creative review with the same authority. There is no one alive better suited to lead Chi+Ko &Tay.

How He Completes the Team

Droga is the bridge. He translates between the creative idealists and the business realists on the team. He sets the vision, protects the culture, recruits the talent, and makes the final call on which clients Chi+Ko &Tay pursues. His personal credibility opens doors that no pitch deck ever could. He hires people smarter than him — and then gets out of their way.

02 · Global Chief Creative Officer
Lee Clow
American · Los Angeles, California
Retired — Former Chairman, TBWA\Media Arts Lab

Why Him

Lee Clow co-created Apple's "1984" — widely considered the greatest advertisement ever made — and then created the "Think Different" campaign that helped save Apple from bankruptcy and redefined what advertising could be for a technology brand. He spent over 30 years building brands with Steve Jobs. He turned Chiat\Day (and later TBWA) into a creative powerhouse. He understands that advertising isn't about selling products — it's about building cultural movements. His "Media Arts" philosophy — that ideas should be designed for any medium — was prophetic by two decades.

How He Completes the Team

Clow is the creative conscience. While Droga runs the business, Clow holds the creative standard. He doesn't chase trends — he creates them. He brings a California surf-culture calm that balances the intensity of the rest of the team. He's worked with the most demanding client in history (Steve Jobs) and made it work for 30 years. That temperament is invaluable. He completes Serpa's visual precision with conceptual grandeur.

Apple "1984" & "Think Different" Cannes Lion of St. Mark TBWA\Media Arts Lab Founder ADC Hall of Fame Clio Lifetime Achievement 30+ Years With Apple
03 · Global Chief Strategy Officer
Jon Steel
British · Started at Boase Massimi Pollitt, London
Retired — Former VP, WPP

Why Him

Jon Steel is — by near-universal consensus — the greatest account planner in advertising history. His book "Truth, Lies & Advertising" is the bible of the discipline. He built the planning department at Goodby, Silverstein & Partners from scratch and grew the agency from $40 million to $776 million. He won 11 gold Effie Awards for effectiveness. He was the strategic mind behind "got milk?" — one of the most iconic campaigns of the 20th century. He was named one of the top five account planners in the world by peers who called him "one of the great practitioners in advertising today." His philosophy: planning exists to create the conditions for great creative work.

How He Completes the Team

Steel is the human-truth engine. While Clow and Serpa push for creative greatness, Steel ensures every idea is rooted in a genuine consumer insight. He bridges the British planning tradition (rigor, research, evidence) with American creative ambition. His belief that planners should spend time with real people — not just data — creates the insights that make great creative inevitable. He gives Droga the strategic ammunition to sell bold work to cautious clients.

"Got Milk?" Strategist 11 Gold Effie Awards AAF Hall of Achievement Adweek West Coast Executive of Year Author: Truth, Lies & Advertising Built GSP Planning Dept
04 · Executive Creative Director — Art
Marcello Serpa
Brazilian-German · São Paulo & Munich
Active — Independent Creative Consultant

Why Him

Brazil's most honored art director in history. First non-Anglo-Saxon to receive the Clio Lifetime Achievement Award. He built AlmapBBDO into a three-time Cannes Agency of the Year nominee and the first-ever double winner of Agency of the Year in the Gunn Report. He turned Havaianas from a cheap rubber sandal into a global lifestyle brand through 17 years of brilliantly simple advertising. His dual German-Brazilian upbringing fused rational discipline with emotional warmth — creating a visual language that transcends culture and language. His mantra: "The product must always be the hero."

How He Completes the Team

Serpa is the visual master. His German training in reduction ("be simple — reduce everything to a single idea") complements Clow's big-idea thinking with visual precision. He proves that brand-building doesn't require a big language — his work crosses borders because it's visually universal. In a team of English and Portuguese speakers, his design-first approach creates work that transcends words. He also brings the Latin American market expertise that Chi+Ko &Tay needs for global reach.

Clio Lifetime Achievement 2008 Cannes Agency of Year (AlmapBBDO) Gunn Report Double Winner Brazil's Most Awarded Art Director Havaianas Global Brand Builder German+Brazilian Dual Creative Education

06 · Head of Brand Design & Identity
Helmut Krone
American · New York City
Deceased (1925–1996) — Former AD, DDB

Why Him

Helmut Krone led the Creative Revolution that changed advertising forever. His "Think Small" ad for Volkswagen — created at DDB with copywriter Julian Koenig — was voted the number one advertisement of the 20th century by Ad Age. His work is in the Museum of Modern Art and the Smithsonian. He didn't just create ads — he invented the modern advertising format. He questioned every formal device: logotypes, headlines, body copy, photography. He created the tension between words and images that still defines how advertising works today. He gave us ads that are witty, understated, and demand the audience's intelligence.

How He Completes the Team

Krone is the foundation. His design philosophy — strip everything to its essence, respect the audience's intelligence, let the idea do the work — is the bedrock on which Chi+Ko &Tay's visual identity is built. While Serpa brings Brazilian warmth and Clow brings California scale, Krone brings New York precision and the intellectual rigor of the original creative revolution. He sets the design standards that every brand identity Chi+Ko &Tay creates must meet. His ghost haunts every layout that's too busy and every headline that tries too hard.

VW "Think Small" — #1 Ad of 20th Century Work in MoMA & Smithsonian ADC Hall of Fame One Club Creative Hall of Fame Led the Creative Revolution Invented Modern Ad Layout

10 · Managing Director / Head of Client Partnership
Mary Wells Lawrence
American · Youngstown, Ohio / New York City
Deceased (1928–2024) — Founder, Wells Rich Greene

Why Her

Mary Wells Lawrence was the first woman to found, own, and run a major advertising agency, and the first female CEO of a company traded on the New York Stock Exchange. She built Wells Rich Greene into one of the most creative and commercially successful agencies of the 1960s and 70s. She created "Plop, plop, fizz, fizz" for Alka-Seltzer, "Flick your Bic," and "I Love New York." She won the Cannes Lions Lifetime Achievement Award in 2020. But what made her legendary was her client relationships: she could charm, persuade, and hold boardroom power with CEOs while protecting creative work. She understood that the best client relationships are personal, deep, and built on mutual respect.

How She Completes the Team

Wells Lawrence is the diplomat. In a team loaded with creative firebands (Clow, Chiat, Hoffman), she's the one who makes clients feel heard, respected, and excited about taking risks. She builds the long-term relationships that sustain the agency. She's the counterbalance to Chiat's intensity — where he charges in, she builds bridges. Her business acumen means she also contributes to agency operations and financial strategy. She and Droga together ensure that Chi+Ko &Tay is creatively ambitious AND commercially sustainable.

First Female CEO on NYSE Founded Wells Rich Greene "I ♥ NY" Era Agency Leader Cannes Lion of St. Mark 2020 Alka-Seltzer, Bic, Braniff Airlines Legendary Client Relationships
11 · Chairman Emeritus / Creative Godfather
Bill Bernbach
American · New York City
Deceased (1911–1982) — Co-Founder, DDB

Why Him

Bill Bernbach started the Creative Revolution. He invented the modern copywriter-art director team. He hired the most talented and overlooked people of his era — Phyllis Robinson, Helmut Krone, George Lois. He created Volkswagen's "Think Small" — the greatest campaign ever made. He proved that advertising could be intelligent, witty, and respectful of the audience. He fundamentally changed the purpose of advertising from shouting at people to having a conversation with them. His agency, DDB, won Network of the Year at Cannes 2025 with 112 Lions — 43 years after his death. That's legacy.

How He Completes the Team

Bernbach is the philosophical foundation. His principle that "The trick isn't knowing what to say. What makes all the difference is the way you say it" is the DNA of every Chi+Ko &Tay brief. He reunites with Krone — his original art director — and together they set the creative standard that the younger team members aspire to. He is the one who reminds everyone that great advertising respects the audience's intelligence. In a room with Droga, Clow, and Chiat, Bernbach is the quiet authority who makes everyone better by simply raising the bar of what's possible.

Started the Creative Revolution Co-Founded DDB VW "Think Small" (Oversaw) Invented Art Director + Copywriter Team Ad Age #1 Ad Person of 20th Century DDB Still Winning 43 Years After His Death
12 · Head of Brand Strategy & Effectiveness
David Ogilvy
British · Born in West Horsley, Surrey
Deceased (1911–1999) — Founder, Ogilvy & Mather

Why Him

David Ogilvy — the "Father of Advertising" — founded Ogilvy & Mather and built it into one of the largest and most respected agencies in the world. His genius was the marriage of research and creativity. He believed advertising had to sell — and he proved it could sell brilliantly. He created the Hathaway shirt man, "The man in the Hathaway shirt," the Rolls-Royce headline ("At 60 miles an hour the loudest noise comes from the electric clock"), and Dove's iconic brand positioning. His books — "Confessions of an Advertising Man" and "Ogilvy on Advertising" — remain the definitive texts on brand-building. His agency won Creative Company of the Year at Cannes 2025.

How He Completes the Team

Ogilvy is the effectiveness backbone. While Bernbach is the creative idealist and Clow is the conceptual visionary, Ogilvy ensures every campaign proves its worth in results. He bridges Steel's consumer insights with hard business outcomes. He writes the effectiveness case studies that win Effies. He reminds the team that creative work that doesn't drive results is just art — and Chi+Ko &Tay is in the business of building brands that sell. His British formality also beautifully counterbalances the American and Brazilian informality on the team.

Founded Ogilvy & Mather "Father of Advertising" Rolls-Royce, Dove, Hathaway Author: Confessions & Ogilvy on Advertising Ogilvy still winning Cannes in 2025 Research-Driven Brand Builder
13 · Chief Culture Officer
Dan Wieden
American · Portland, Oregon
Deceased (1945–2022) — Co-Founder, Wieden+Kennedy

Why Him

Dan Wieden wrote "Just Do It." Three words that became a global cultural movement, transformed Nike into a $50 billion brand, and redefined what advertising could mean to a company. But his real genius was building Wieden+Kennedy — the world's largest independent advertising agency — on a culture so powerful that it defines the company 40+ years later. He codified independence in a trust so the agency could never be sold. He created the principles "Fail Harder," "Walk In Stupid Every Morning," and "Hire Wrong." He built The Kennedys residency program. He made Portland — not New York — the creative capital of American advertising. He didn't just build an agency. He built a creative religion.

How He Completes the Team

Wieden is the soul. While Droga runs the business and Chiat wins the clients, Wieden builds the culture that makes everything else possible. He designs the Chi+Ko &Tay Fellowship program, the creative sabbaticals, the hiring philosophy, the physical space. He's the one who reminds everyone: "Create a culture so weird and wild and sticky that it would hurt your very soul to leave." He and Hoffman — his longtime colleague — bring the W+K DNA directly into Chi+Ko &Tay. He also keeps the agency independent. Forever.

15 · Head of Behavioral Science & Persuasion
Rory Sutherland
British · Vice Chairman, Ogilvy UK
Active — Writer, Speaker, TED Talker

Why Him

Rory Sutherland is the advertising industry's resident genius on behavioral economics, psychology, and irrational human decision-making. As Vice Chairman of Ogilvy UK, he founded the behavioral science practice that has influenced how the entire industry thinks about persuasion. His TED talks have been viewed millions of times. His book "Alchemy" is a masterclass on why people do what they do — and how brands can use that understanding. He argues that the biggest advertising breakthroughs come not from data or technology, but from understanding the wonderful irrationality of human behavior.

How He Completes the Team

Sutherland is the secret weapon. While Steel provides the consumer insight and Ogilvy provides the brand framework, Sutherland adds the layer of behavioral science that makes campaigns not just beautiful but behaviorally effective. He's the one who spots the counterintuitive insight — the one that makes creative teams gasp and clients nervous, but that works because it's true to how humans actually behave. He also brings wit, erudition, and a gift for persuasion that makes him invaluable in client presentations. He can sell the logic behind illogical-seeming ideas better than anyone alive.

Why this team works.

Fifteen people, nine countries, five languages, one shared obsession: making the best work in the world. Here's why the chemistry is explosive.

The Tension Between Rigor & Madness

Ogilvy and Bernbach famously disagreed on everything — research vs. instinct, selling vs. art. Putting them in the same room creates the productive tension that produces work that is both creatively brave AND commercially effective. Steel and Sutherland add layers of evidence. Clow and Hoffman add fearless creative instinct. The work that survives both fires is the work that wins.

Three Art Director Lineages

Krone (New York precision), Serpa (Brazilian-German reduction), and Cheong (Asian immaculate craft) — three entirely different visual traditions that together give Chi+Ko &Tay the ability to create work that resonates in any culture, any language, any market. No other team on earth has this visual range.

The CEO-CCO Powercouple

Droga (CEO) + Clow (CCO) is the dream partnership. Droga understands creative and business equally. Clow understands creative at the highest level but leaves business to others. They won't step on each other's toes. They'll amplify each other's strengths.

Culture × Strategy × Behavioral Science

Yakob reads the culture. Steel reads the consumer. Sutherland reads the irrational mind. Together, they produce strategic briefs that are culturally relevant, humanly true, and behaviorally effective — the holy trinity that makes creative work not just beautiful but unstoppable.

The Fire + The Diplomat

Jay Chiat (relentless, uncompromising, intense) and Mary Wells Lawrence (charming, strategic, relationship-first) form the ultimate client partnership. Chiat wins the pitch. Wells Lawrence keeps the client for 20 years. They balance each other perfectly.

Wieden's Culture Protects Everything

Dan Wieden doesn't create ads. He creates the conditions for everyone else to create the best ads of their lives. His culture — independence, risk-taking, creative freedom, diversity — is the invisible infrastructure that makes every other person on this list perform at their peak. He's the reason they'll all stay.
