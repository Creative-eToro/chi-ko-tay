02 — Founding Philosophy

Seven beliefs that
make us different.

Drawing from the DNA of every agency that has shaped history — from DDB's creative revolution to W+K's independence-first model — these are the non-negotiable principles encoded into Chi+Ko &Tay's foundation.

The Work Comes First

Borrowed directly from Wieden+Kennedy's founding mantra. Not politics, not revenue, not ego. The quality of the creative product is the single highest priority. Great work attracts great clients. Not the other way around.

Strategy Is Creative

The false divide between strategy and creative is destroyed. At Chi+Ko &Tay, strategists sit beside creatives from brief to execution. Every campaign is born from a human truth so sharp it becomes the creative springboard. This is how you win Cannes — and move markets.

Fail Harder

Another W+K principle we adopt without shame. Safe work is invisible work. We invest in bold ideas, kill them fast if they don't work, and celebrate the risk-taking that produced them. The graveyard of great failed ideas is the soil for breakthrough work.

Fiercely Independent

No holding company will ever own Chi+Ko &Tay. Independence is codified in the founding charter. It lets us hire misfits, fire bad clients, take creative risks, and make decisions based on conviction — not quarterly earnings reports.

Culture Before Scale

We open new offices only when the culture can be transplanted intact. Growth for growth's sake is the enemy of creative excellence. DDB won 112 Lions in 2025 not because they were big, but because their creative council maintained standards across every office.

Emotional Storytelling Moves Markets

Every piece of data we touch, every strategy we write, every campaign we produce is in service of one thing: making people feel something. The brands that own an emotion own their category. We build emotional monopolies.

03 — Organizational Architecture

Designed for creative
velocity.

Chi+Ko &Tay uses a hybrid matrix-pod structure. Traditional departments provide depth and specialization. Cross-functional pods — assembled per client engagement — provide speed and integration. No silos. No gatekeepers. Just proximity between talent and problems.

The Pod System

Each major client gets a dedicated pod: a strategist, creative team, producer, data lead, and account director working as a single unit. The pod operates like a startup within the agency — autonomous, accountable, fast.

The Creative Council

Modeled on DDB's award-winning Bullseye program. Senior creative leaders across all offices convene weekly to review work, push standards, identify award-worthy campaigns, and mentor rising talent. This is the quality engine.

The Lab

A dedicated R&D unit exploring emerging platforms, AI tools, immersive experiences, and new storytelling formats. 10% of agency revenue funds The Lab. Its discoveries feed directly into client work — giving Chi+Ko &Tay a structural innovation advantage.

10.5 — The Cannes Lions Strategy

Engineering awards
dominance.

Winning Cannes Lions is not luck. It requires deliberate infrastructure. Here's how Chi+Ko &Tay ensures consistent award performance year after year — based on how DDB, Ogilvy, Publicis Conseil, and WPP engineered their wins.

Dedicated Awards Director

A full-time hire who manages the entire awards calendar: Cannes, D&AD, One Show, Clios, Effies, Webbys. They identify award-worthy work early, coordinate submissions, manage case study production, and ensure proper category strategy.

Work Grading System

The Creative Council grades every campaign on a 1–5 scale. Only Level 4 and 5 work gets submitted to awards. Level 3 work gets pushed back to the team with a mandate to elevate. This creates a culture where award-level quality is the baseline expectation.

Pro-Bono & Passion Projects

Chi+Ko &Tay dedicates 10% of creative hours to pro-bono work for social causes and NGOs. This work is a talent magnet, a creative playground, and historically the source of Cannes Grand Prix wins (Dove's Real Beauty work for Ogilvy spans two decades).

Category Diversification

Don't just enter Film and Print. Chi+Ko &Tay systematically targets underserved categories: Creative Strategy, Creative Effectiveness, Innovation, Creative Data, and Entertainment. This diversification maximizes total Lions count and surfaces the breadth of the agency's capabilities.

Case Study Production Team

Two dedicated case study producers who create the 2-minute entry films, results decks, and written cases for every award submission. A mediocre case study kills a great campaign's chances. Chi+Ko &Tay treats case study production as a creative discipline in itself.

Jury Intelligence

Study jury compositions, past jury preferences, and winning patterns across every award show. Brief creative teams on category trends and judging criteria. Send senior leaders to serve on juries whenever possible — it builds relationships and sharpens standards.

11 — Culture & Talent

A place so alive
it hurts to leave.

Dan Wieden said it best: "Create a culture so weird and wild and sticky that it would hurt your very soul to leave." Chi+Ko &Tay's culture isn't a perk package — it's the competitive advantage that attracts talent, retains talent, and produces the work.

01

Hire Wrong

Recruit outside the ad industry. Hire poets, filmmakers, behavioral scientists, anthropologists, game designers, journalists, musicians. The best ideas come from the collision of different perspectives. Chi+Ko &Tay's hiring process values curiosity and craft over industry pedigree.

02

Walk In Stupid Every Morning

Another W+K principle. The moment you think you know everything, you stop learning. Chi+Ko &Tay rewards intellectual humility and relentless curiosity. Every client challenge is approached with fresh eyes, not template solutions.

03

The Chi+Ko &Tay Fellowship

A paid 6-month creative residency program for 12 emerging talents from non-traditional backgrounds. Modeled on W+K's Kennedys program. Fellows work on real client briefs, receive mentorship from senior creatives, and are fast-tracked into full-time roles. This is the talent pipeline.

04

Creative Sabbaticals

After 3 years, every employee gets a 4-week paid sabbatical to pursue a personal creative project — film, art, travel, writing, anything. They present what they learned when they return. This prevents burnout and feeds new ideas back into the agency.

05

No Pitch Theater

Chi+Ko &Tay doesn't do speculative pitches for free. We present our thinking, our credentials, and our people. Clients who demand free creative spec work aren't the right clients. This protects creative energy and signals confidence.

06

Fire Bad Clients

Independence means the power to walk away. If a client doesn't respect the work, doesn't trust the agency, or creates a toxic environment for the team — they're fired. This is non-negotiable and the single biggest reason talent will choose Chi+Ko &Tay over holding company agencies.

07

Diversity Is Not a Program

Diversity of thought, background, culture, and experience is embedded in hiring, not siloed in an initiative. The Cultural Intelligence team ensures global perspectives inform every brief. The Fellowship specifically targets underrepresented voices. Leadership reflects the diversity of the markets Chi+Ko &Tay serves.

08

Compensation That Competes

Chi+Ko &Tay pays top-of-market salaries — period. Creative talent shouldn't have to choose between doing great work and earning a great living. Equity participation for senior hires. Profit-sharing for all employees after Year 2. The best talent goes where they're valued, creatively and financially.
